## Why Maintenance Matters

Mud, dirt, and trail debris wear down components like chains, brakes, and suspension, causing expensive repairs if neglected. Well-maintained bikes deliver reliable handling and extended component lifespan. Your MTB always signals when it needs attention — squeaks, slips, and wobbles are its language.

## Post-Ride Routine (15 Minutes)

### 1. Rinse the Bike (3 min)

Use a garden hose with moderate pressure — avoid pressure washers, which force water past seals. Rinse top to bottom:
- Frame and fork
- Shock and wheels
- Drivetrain
- Underside of downtube
- Linkage areas (full-suspension bikes)
- Bottom bracket zone

For caked mud, let water soak briefly before rinsing. A soft brush helps dislodge stubborn debris.

### 2. Chain Clean and Lube (4 min)

A dirty chain shifts poorly, wears faster, and damages cassettes and chainrings.

**Quick clean:** Run a clean rag around the chain while backpedalling.

**Deeper clean:** Use a chain cleaning device with degreaser every few rides.

**Lubrication:** Apply one drop of lube per link while slowly backpedalling. Backpedal a dozen times to work it in, then wipe excess with a clean rag.

**Lube selection lifehack:**
- Wet lube for wet/muddy conditions — stays longer but attracts dirt
- Dry lube for dry conditions — sheds dirt better but washes off easily
- Less is more: too much lube just attracts more grit

### 3. Brake Pad Inspection (2 min)

Look through the top of each brake caliper. Check friction material thickness — most pads have a wear indicator groove.
- Replace when worn to 1–1.5 mm remaining
- Check rotor scoring by running a finger lightly along the braking surface when cool
- Spongy lever or lever reaching the bar = worn pads or air in the hydraulic system

### 4. Tyre Inspection (2 min)

Run hands around tread and sidewall:
- **Embedded debris:** Remove thorns, flints, or wire fragments immediately
- **Cuts and gashes:** Deep cuts exposing the casing need replacement soon
- **Sidewall condition:** Bulging sidewalls = compromised integrity
- **Knob wear:** Excessively worn knobs reduce grip in mud and corners
- Check pressure with a gauge — tubeless tyres lose 1–2 PSI between rides, especially in cold weather

### 5. Bolt Check (2 min)

Quick squeeze and wiggle — not a full re-torque, just confirming nothing loosened:
- Handlebar and stem bolts
- Wheel axles (lift and rock side-to-side)
- Saddle and seatpost / dropper collar
- Brake caliper mounts
- Crank bolts (rock each arm laterally)

**Lifehack:** Use a torque wrench for stem bolts and carbon components — over-tightening damages aluminum and carbon.

### 6. Suspension Wipe (2 min)

Wipe exposed fork stanchion tubes with a clean, soft cloth. Remove dirt and grit that would otherwise drag past seals into fork internals. Do the same for the rear shock shaft. Weeping oil or drips = seal failure needing attention.

## Weekly / Every 3–5 Rides

- **Drivetrain assessment:** Check for shark-fin-shaped teeth on chainring or cassette (sign of wear). Use a chain wear tool — replace chain at 0.5–0.75% wear and your cassette/chainring will last 2–3x longer.
- **Wheel check:** Spin each wheel and watch rim alignment relative to brake pads. Squeeze pairs of spokes — all should feel similar in tension.
- **Suspension air pressure:** Fork pressure deflates naturally over time — check bi-weekly.
- **Pivot bearings (full suspension):** Rock rear wheel side-to-side while holding frame. Any knocking or play beyond the hub = worn pivot bearings.
- **Inspect brake pads:** Replace if thinner than 1–2 mm.
- **Torque bolts:** Go through all fasteners with a torque wrench.

## Maintenance Schedule by Interval

| Frequency | Tasks |
|---|---|
| After every ride | Rinse bike, wipe chain, apply light lube, check tire pressure, tighten axles, wipe suspension stanchions |
| Every 3–5 rides | Inspect brake pads, check suspension air pressure, measure chain wear, torque bolts, inspect tires |
| Every 50 hours | Lower leg fork service: clean/re-grease bushings, replace dust seals and foam rings, refill oil |
| Every 3–6 months | Service fork lowers, bleed brakes if needed, deep-clean drivetrain, refresh tubeless sealant, inspect frame |
| Every 100–150 hours | Full suspension service: fork damper, rear shock, brake bleed, headset, bottom bracket, hubs, drivetrain |
| Every 6–12 months | Service rear shock, full frame inspection (bearings, headset, bottom bracket) |
| Annually | Full overhaul: pivot bearing replacement, cable/hose replacement, frame inspection, wheel rebuild assessment |

## Key Lifehacks & Pro Tips

- **Track your ride hours** using a GPS unit or app (Strava, TrailForks) to schedule service based on actual use, not just calendar time.
- **Avoid high-pressure washers** — they force water past seals and into bearings.
- **Store your bike indoors** or in a sheltered area. Use hooks for elevation. UV, rain, and humidity cause rust and deterioration.
- **Tubeless sealant dries out** — top it up every 2–3 months or after a puncture repair.
- **Less chain lube is more** — the goal is lubrication inside the chain links, not a shiny coat outside.
- **Use manufacturer-recommended products** for suspension lubrication — wrong products degrade seals.
- **Lockout compression** should only be used on roads or smooth singletrack — active suspension helps traction on climbs too.
- **Build the 15-minute habit** consistently — small regular effort dramatically extends component life and reduces costly repairs.
- **When to go to a shop:** Persistent gear skipping after cleaning, suspension leaks, wobbly wheels or bent rotors that resist DIY fixes.

## Component Replacement Guidelines

| Component | Replace When |
|---|---|
| Chain | 0.5–0.75% wear (use chain wear tool) |
| Brake pads | Less than 1–2 mm of friction material remaining |
| Cassette / chainring | After 2–3 chains, or when teeth are shark-fin shaped |
| Fork lower leg oil | Every 50 hours |
| Rear shock full service | Every 100–150 hours or if wheezing/clunking |
| Tubeless sealant | Every 2–3 months |
| Hydraulic brake fluid | When fluid darkens |
| Carbon components | Every 3–6 years |
